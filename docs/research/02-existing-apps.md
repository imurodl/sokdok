# 02 — Existing Korean reading apps & general fluency tools

> Research question: What platforms teach Korean reading (esp. fluency/speed), and what method does each use? Where are the gaps vs. Chinese/Japanese?

## Gaps: exists for Chinese/Japanese but NOT Korean

1. **Du Chinese** — polished interactive graded reader, 1,500+ stories, deep vocab context. Korean's closest is TTMIK Stories, but less integrated.
2. **The Chairman's Bao** — graded reader built from *real current news*. **No Korean equivalent found.**
3. **Satori Reader** — dedicated intermediate-gap immersion app w/ audio shadowing. **No Korean equivalent.**
4. **Explicit reading-speed measurement / RSVP for Korean** — none found.
5. **Per-word karaoke highlight synced to audio** — TTMIK does sentence-level only.

## Bottom line

The Korean market is saturated with **graded-reader + audio** apps and **TOPIK exam-prep** apps. **No Korean app measures or trains reading speed** as its core value, and **none bridges the graded→native cliff** with leveled native content. The general-purpose speed tools that do target speed (RSVP, Beeline) are debunked or unproven. That is the open lane.
