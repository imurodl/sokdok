# Sokdok — Research Index

Research conducted 2026-07-14 via a swarm of parallel web-research agents, to answer:
**Why do intermediate Korean learners (TOPIK 3–5) read slowly, what actually fixes it, does a tool for it already exist, and what would it take to build one?**

The same question generalizes to any known-but-under-practiced script (e.g. Cyrillic for a Latin-dominant reader).

## One-paragraph conclusion

Slow reading at TOPIK 3–5 is **not a decoding problem** (Hangul is phonologically transparent) and **not solvable by "speed-reading" tricks** (RSVP, eye-span training, and subvocalization-elimination are debunked / physiologically impossible). It is an **automaticity + exposure problem**: fast readers recognize frequent words and multi-word chunks effortlessly, and the only proven path there is large volumes of *engaged, level-appropriate, measured* reading — best accelerated by **Extensive Reading + Timed Repeated Reading + Reading-While-Listening**. No existing Korean app measures or trains reading *speed*, and none bridges the "graded-reader → native-content" cliff. That is the gap Sokdok fills.
